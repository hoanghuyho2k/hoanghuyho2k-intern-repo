
# Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) – Laptop Ergonomics

## Risks of using a laptop without proper setup

Using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard can lead to poor posture because the screen and keyboard are attached. This often causes:
- neck strain from looking down at the screen
- back pain from slouching
- wrist discomfort from improper typing angles
- eye strain from prolonged screen exposure

Over time, these issues can lead to long-term musculoskeletal problems and reduced productivity.

## Ergonomic equipment to improve posture

Several tools can improve a laptop-based workspace:
- Laptop stand to raise the screen to eye level
- External keyboard and mouse for better hand positioning
- Ergonomic chair with back support
- External monitor for a larger and adjustable display
- Wrist support or mouse pad for comfort

## Recommended workspace adjustments

To maintain a healthy posture:
- The top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level
- The chair should support the lower back and allow feet to rest flat on the floor
- Elbows should be at a 90-degree angle when typing
- The keyboard and mouse should be positioned close to the body
- The screen should be about an arm’s length away

## Daily habits to reduce strain

Good habits are essential for long-term comfort:
- Take regular breaks (e.g., every 30–60 minutes)
- Stretch and move around frequently
- Follow the 20-20-20 rule (look 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes)
- Maintain an upright posture and avoid slouching
- Blink regularly to reduce eye dryness

## Reflection

### Equipment changes

I adjusted my workspace by raising my laptop to a higher position using a stand. This helped bring the screen closer to eye level and reduced neck strain. I also identified that using an external keyboard would further improve my posture and typing comfort.

### Behaviour changes

I made a conscious effort to sit upright instead of leaning forward. I also started taking short breaks regularly to stretch and move, which helped reduce stiffness and fatigue.

### Reminders and habits

To maintain consistency, I used reminders to take movement breaks throughout the day. These reminders helped me stay aware of my posture and avoid long periods of sitting without movement.

### Workspace improvement

After making these changes, I noticed improved comfort while working and less strain on my neck and shoulders. Small adjustments and consistent habits made a noticeable difference in my overall productivity and wellbeing.
