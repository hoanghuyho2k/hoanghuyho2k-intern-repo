
# Agile Principles & Frameworks

## What is Agile?

Agile is a flexible approach to project management that focuses on:
- continuous improvement
- collaboration
- delivering value quickly

Unlike traditional project management (Waterfall), Agile:
- adapts to change instead of following a fixed plan
- delivers work incrementally instead of all at once
- emphasizes communication and feedback

## Core Values of Agile (Agile Manifesto)

The Agile Manifesto highlights:
- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools  
- Working software over comprehensive documentation  
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation  
- Responding to change over following a plan  

## Scrum vs Kanban

### Scrum
- work is organized into fixed time periods (sprints)
- roles are clearly defined (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Developers)
- includes structured ceremonies (planning, stand-ups, retrospectives)

### Kanban
- work flows continuously without fixed sprints
- tasks are managed on a board and updated in real-time
- focuses on limiting work in progress (WIP)
- more flexible and adaptable

## Why Focus Bear uses Kanban

Focus Bear leans towards Kanban because:
- it allows continuous delivery of tasks
- it is flexible and adapts easily to changes
- it fits well with asynchronous and remote work
- it avoids strict sprint deadlines

## Reflection

### Benefits of Agile

- flexibility to adapt to changes
- faster delivery of features
- better communication within teams
- continuous improvement through feedback

### Challenges of Agile

- requires strong communication
- can be difficult to manage without discipline
- priorities may change frequently
- less structure compared to traditional methods

### Applying Agile in my role

As a backend developer intern, Agile helps me:
- work on small, manageable tasks
- update progress regularly
- respond to feedback quickly
- improve my work continuously

### Scrum vs Kanban (in my own words)

Scrum is more structured with fixed timelines and defined roles, while Kanban is more flexible and focuses on continuous workflow and task movement.

### Most useful Agile principle

The most useful Agile principle for me is:

**Responding to change over following a plan**

This is important because tasks and requirements can change during development, and being flexible helps me adapt and deliver better results.

## Key Learning

Agile promotes flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement. Understanding Agile principles helps me work more effectively and adapt to changing requirements in a team environment.
